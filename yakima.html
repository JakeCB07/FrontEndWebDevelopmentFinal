The Palm Springs of Washington

Yakima is a bustling urban city built by agriculture. The rich soil of the Yakima Valley made it an ideal location for growing all manner of crops. The Union Gap area of Yakima has become the city's central shopping area, which is home to the Yakima Mall. Many events such as the Central Washington State Fair are held at the Yakima Valley Sun Dome, a 85,500 square foot stone dome near the downtown area of Yakima. Yakima has many outdoor activities too, such as camping in the forests to the west and fishing along the Yakima river.

Geography

The city of Yakima is located in south-central Washington in the Yakima Valley. The city of Yakima was built along the Yakima river. Despite the large presence of agriculture in the city, the climate is considered to be a semi-arid desert. Summer months typically get as hot as 90°F. The winter month usually range form 20°F to 30°F. Yakima has an average precipitation of 8 inches per year.

Demographics

Despite American settlers inhabiting the Yakima Valley since 1847, Yakima wasn't officially incorporated until 1883. Yakima is home to a population of 256,035 residents as of 2021. The official Yakima government website describes Yakima as a "diverse metropolitan city", which would classify it as an urban city. As of 2021, the largest ethnic group in Yakima is hispanics and latinos, making up 50.2% of the population. The next largest ethnicity is white, which is 42.3% of the population. The third largest group is native american which makes up 6.7% of the total population.

Economy

The primary industry of Yakima is agriculture. According to the Employment Security Department of Washington state, in 2020 the total payroll for agriculture, forestry and fishing workers was $1,101,121,332 dollars. The next largest industry was health services, having a total payroll of $784,947,681 dollars in 2020. The largest following industry after health services was local goverment, which had a total payroll of $716,410,229 in the same year. The average yearly income for an employed Yakima resident as of 2020 was $44,223. This is 43.4% lower than the average Washingtonian, who had an average yearly income of $76,801 in 2020.
