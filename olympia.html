The State Capital

Olympia is the quaint but lovely capital of Washington state. There's plenty to do here. Take a tour of the capitol building or go for a stroll on the boardwalk. Just a couple blocks from the capitol building is the Puget Sound Estuarium, where you can gaze at the creatures that inhabit the Salish sea. Looking for a place to take the kids? Why not take them to Hands-On Children's Museum? There are over 150 fun exhibits and activities to participate in. Olympia has plenty to explore and discover!

Geography

The city of Olympia resides on the south end of the Puget Sound, which resides in north-western Washington. The land around the southern Puget Sound contains thick forrests. Olympia has a mediterranean climate, causing it to have warm and dry summers ass well as cold and wet winters. The city of Olympia receives most of it precipitation from October to March, averaging 9.6 inches of rain per year. The summer temperatures in Olympia can get as high as 78°F and the winter temperature can get as low as 34°F.

Demographics

Olympia was named the capital of the Washington territories in 1853. Olympia was officially incorporated as a town in 1859. According to the Data USA, Olympia had a population of 54,579 in the year 2021. The official Thurston County government website classifies Olympia as an urban city. Economy

Of its 54,579 residents, 26,500 of them are employed. Due to it being the capital of Washington, the largest industry in Olympia is Public Administration which employs 4,603 people. The next largest industry is Healthcare and Social Assistance, employing 3,773 residents. The income for the average Olympian is $45,028 in 2021, which was about 45% lower than the state average. The typical Washingtonian made an average of $81,245 according to the Washington State Office of Financial Management in the same year. Olympia's primary domestic trade product is miscellaneous materials and supplies, bringing in $42.2 billion dollars annually.
