Welcome to the Evergreen State

Washington is the 42nd state in the United States of America. Washington is widely known for its gorgeous landscape. It's home to soothing lakes, towering mountain tops, and lush forests. Washington's temperate climate also makes it perfect for agriculture. Washington state produces a large variety of fruits and vegetables. Washington also has a rich and storied history. Continue reading to learn more about our beautiful state!

Tourism

Washington is full of recreational activities that any outdoors enthusiast would love! For the adrenaline seekers, we have skiing at Snoqualmie National Forest or kayaking at Lake Washington. For those looking for quiet getaway, try fishing at the Walla Walla river or camping at Mount Rainier national park? Not the outdoors-ey type? Then take a trip to Seattle! There's no shortage of tourist traps. Visit the 605 ft tall wonder that is the Seattle Space Needle. Or if you aren't a fan of heights, head over to the Woodland Park Zoo for some family-friendly fun. When you're planning your next vacation, be sure to keep Washington in mind!

Exports

Washington exports a significant amount of produce every year. Some of Washingtons biggest produce exports include apples, cherries, and potatoes. In fact, according to the U.S. Census Bureau, Washington state exported $7.5 billion dollars worth of produce and processed foods. Also according to the U.S. Census Bureau, Washington exports a significant amount of a transportation equipment. Most of this transportation equipment is airplanes and airplane parts comming from large aerospace companies stationed in Washington. Two of these big aerospace companies include Boeing and TLG Aerospace.

History

Washington didn't become a state until 1889. However, this doesn't mean nothing notable happened before then in terms of U.S. history. In 1805, Lewis and Clark walked along the Snake and Columbia rivers on their expedition across North America. In 1926, Bertha Knight Landes became the first female mayor of a major U.S. city. Bertha undertook the task of attempting to clean up Seattle, which was rife with illegal gambling and drinking at the time. In more recent history, 1980 marks the devastating eruption of Mount St. Helens, where 57 lives were lost and over $1 billion dollars in damages occurred.
